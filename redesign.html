Project YouTube Redesign
Type Interface Redesign
Year 2025
Role UI/UX Designer
/ Overview (01)

The world's largest video platform — redesigned to actually respect the people watching it.

YouTube's interface has accumulated years of competing priorities — monetisation, recommendations, shorts, ads — layered on top of each other without a coherent visual system underneath. This conceptual redesign audits the core viewing experience and rebuilds it around one principle: the video should always win. The project covers the homepage, watch page, and search results — the three screens where friction is highest and attention is most fragile.

/ Design Decisions (02)
Scroll to explore
01

Visual Hierarchy Collapse

Thumbnails, ads, and Shorts all compete at the same visual weight.

The homepage treats every piece of content identically — a 3-minute vlog, a 2-hour documentary, and a sponsored post all share the same card size, the same thumbnail ratio, and the same label treatment. The eye has nowhere to land.

A tiered card system — large editorial cards for long-form content, compact rows for shorts, and a clear visual separator for promoted content. Hierarchy is restored without removing any content type.
02

Shorts Immersion Break

Reading comments shouldn't shrink the experience.

In the current UI, opening the comment section on a Short minimizes the video into a rigid, floating box. It disrupts the full-screen vertical immersion completely just to display text.

A seamless, transparent comment overlay. The rigid bounding box is removed, allowing the video to continue playing edge-to-edge in the background while the UI remains clean and open.
03

Search Result Noise

Search returns a mix of videos, ads, playlists, and Shorts with no clear distinction.

A query for a tutorial returns paid promotions, algorithmically recommended tangents, and unrelated Shorts in the same list as the actual result — with no visual or structural way to tell them apart at a glance.

Search results are grouped by content type with a sticky filter bar: Videos, Playlists, Channels, Shorts. Promoted content is labelled clearly and visually offset — present but not disguised.
04

Curation & Feed Clarity

Playlists are rigid, and subscription feeds lack chronological focus.

Standard playlists make it tedious to categorize saved videos. Meanwhile, the Subscriptions tab can easily get overwhelmed by high-volume uploaders, burying content from other creators you follow.

A new customizable "Collections" system for fluid video categorization, paired with an overhauled, timeline-based Subscription feed. Videos are strictly grouped by day, ensuring you never miss an upload.
05

History Page Navigation

Finding a video from three days ago shouldn't be a scavenger hunt.

The current history page is an endless, mixed dump of 10-second Shorts, random clicks, and 2-hour podcasts. Finding a specific past video relies entirely on endless scrolling and visual memory.

A structured, timeline-based History architecture. Days are clearly separated, and fast-paced Shorts are filtered into their own distinct horizontal rows, keeping the main vertical feed clean for long-form content.
06

Watch Page Context

Live chats and recommendations fight for the exact same real estate.

During live streams or premieres, the screen becomes a chaotic mess. Users are forced to choose between engaging with the community or discovering what to watch next, as both sections clash visually.

Integrated toggle tabs built directly beside the player. Users can seamlessly switch between the "Live Chat" view and the "Up Next" recommendations within the same clean side-panel, keeping the viewport tidy.
