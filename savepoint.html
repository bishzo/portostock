Project SAVEPOINT
Type Mobile App
Year 2024
Role UI/UX Designer
/ Overview (01)

A social platform for gamers — built for the people who play seriously but had nowhere to share it.

SAVEPOINT is a React Native mobile application designed for gamers who want more than a leaderboard. Built to bridge the gap between solo play and community, it lets users track their gaming progress, share milestones, and connect with a squad — without leaving the experience of the game itself behind. Designed solo across UI and UX in 2024.

/ Design Decisions (02)
Scroll to explore
01

Fleeting Social Feeds

Gaming clips disappear down fast-moving, disposable timelines.

Traditional platforms treat gameplay as disposable content. Gamers lack a dedicated space focused on preserving their personal digital history and virtual memories.

Structured as a digital journal, the profile grid and categorized tabs create a permanent, celebratory archive rather than just another fleeting feed.
02

Hyper-Competitive Aesthetics

Aggressive neons and "Twitch-style" UI alienate casual players.

Most gaming apps rely heavily on heavy blues and purples catering to a hyper-competitive mindset. This visual noise creates an exhausting environment for relaxed players.

A premium, editorial cream-and-black palette intentionally shifts the brand focus toward warmth, nostalgia, and personal memory.
03

Unreliable Media Uploads

Standard apps struggle with large, high-quality gameplay videos.

Relying on standard Base64 encoding inflates file sizes, eats up memory, and frequently causes large video uploads to fail for the end user.

Engineering bypasses Base64 entirely. Direct binary streaming to Supabase Storage prevents inflation and ensures large media is handled efficiently and reliably.
04

Low Archiving Motivation

Users lose motivation to document moments without validation.

Without immediate feedback or incentives, the habit of archiving experiences quickly drops off, leading to low long-term engagement and retention.

Built-in gamification awards XP for posting and engaging. Streaks and leaderboards turn memory archiving into a rewarding, community-driven loop.
05

Sluggish Interactions

Waiting for network confirmations makes core social features clunky.

Complex database queries cause noticeable UI lag when liking, commenting, or messaging, disrupting the instantaneous feel expected from modern social apps.

Optimistic UI instantly injects actions into local state. Client-side data joining keeps chats and feeds feeling instantaneous before the network even catches up.
