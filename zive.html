Project ZIVE
Type Brand Strategy
Year 2025
Role UX Research & Brand
/ Overview (01)

A booking platform built for travelers who need to actually find where they're going — not just where they're staying.

ZIVE is a curated micro-space and capsule hotel booking platform designed for the modern solo traveler in Japan. The project began as a research exercise into why existing platforms fail international guests — and turned into a full brand and UX strategy deliverable. Competitor audit, three user personas, a mood board, and eight specific pain-point-to-solution mappings were produced as the foundation for any future build.

/ Pain Points & Solutions (02)
Scroll to explore
01

Language Barrier

Listings feel unreliable before you even book.

Amenity descriptions and check-in instructions written only in Japanese — or poorly machine-translated — leave international travelers guessing what they're actually booking.

Every ZIVE listing uses universal iconography and requires bilingual descriptions. Filter tags work in both languages so nothing is lost in translation.
02

Navigation

You don't know where you're going until you're already lost.

"Inside Shinjuku Station" with no exit name or landmark is an anxiety trigger, not a confirmation. You find out it's confusing when you're already there.

Every listing has a "How to Find Us" card — exit name, walking time, landmark photo, address in English and Japanese. Resent 24 hours before check-in.
03

Pricing Transparency

The real price only appears at the last step.

Platforms show a nightly rate then add taxes, service fees, and cleaning charges at checkout. By then you're already invested — and either annoyed or just paying it.

ZIVE shows the full price — taxes and fees included — from the moment you open a listing. The number on the card is the number you pay.
04

Capsule Clarity

You can't tell what the capsule is actually like.

Whether there's a door or just a curtain, how tall the bunk is, whether the locker fits a laptop bag — none of it is standardised or reliably listed.

Granular filter tags: door vs. curtain, bunk height in cm, noise level rating, locker size. Every detail that affects comfort is searchable before you open a single listing.
05

Listing Photography

Photos are misleading or missing entirely.

Wide-angle distortion, stylised renders, or one blurry exterior shot. You book based on what looks good, arrive to something different, and lose trust in the platform.

Every ZIVE listing requires a minimum set of honest, natural-light photos — pod interior, desk, bathroom, locker, entrance. No renders, no distortion.
06

Payment Friction

Foreign cards get rejected with no explanation.

You reach payment, enter your card, and it fails. No clear reason, no alternative suggested. You either abandon the booking or spend time troubleshooting.

ZIVE supports major international cards, PayPal, and regional methods. The system detects your location and surfaces the most likely working option first.
07

Host Communication

Host communication is slow, automated, and impersonal.

Pre-arrival instructions arrive as a wall of text the morning of check-in — too late to be useful, too dense to read quickly.

In-app messaging with pre-translated templates. A personalised pre-arrival message sent 24 hours before check-in — concise, structured, human in tone.
08

Work-Readiness

Finding a work-friendly stay means opening twelve tabs.

"Business-friendly" means nothing specific. You cross-reference listing descriptions, hunt for a desk in photos, google the Wi-Fi speed, and still aren't sure until you arrive.

ZIVE's Work-Ready badge is verified against fixed criteria — desk present, Wi-Fi speed tested, noise rating, power outlet visible in photos. One badge, no guesswork.
